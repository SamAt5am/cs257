A cryptic crossword puzzle is a variation on the classic crossword, but with extra tricky clues!

While the standard New York Times-style crossword is more popular in the US, cryptic crosswords are more popular overseas. In a cryptic crossword, clues take on a very particular structure:

One part of the clue is a literal definition of the intended answer. The other part is a super elaborate pun meant to hint at the answer. Consider the following clue, which appeared in a 2019 puzzle from the New York Times Magazine:

"Not up to par, truant soldier retreats into drink (5,7)"

The answer is BELOWAVERAGE. "Not up to par" is a literal clue for BELOWAVERAGE. However, the rest of the clue is far more devious. A truant soldier can be described as "AWOL", and "drink" is another word for "beverage." If we insert the acronym AWOL backwards into the word BEVERAGE, we get BELOWAVERAGE, so a truant soldier is "retreating into drink."

Fun!
